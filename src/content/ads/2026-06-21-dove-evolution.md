template: |
  1. Locate the standard the audience is silently measuring themselves against. The one they would never say out loud.
  2. Acquire visual evidence of how that standard was manufactured. Not described. Filmed.
  3. Build a piece of media where the manufacturing process is the entire narrative. Do not argue against the standard. Show it being made.
  4. Do not show your product. The point is not the product. The point is the standard.
  5. Let the audience walk away with the standard collapsed. The brand inherits the relief.
appliedExample: |
  Two examples. One in a different category to prove the template generalizes. One in the actual brand it is about to be used for to prove it fits.

  **A. Different category: an honest journaling app.**

  Most self-help apps show transformation success stories. The Dove move is to show the manufacturing of those stories.

  Ninety seconds. A still camera. A woman sitting at a kitchen table reading her morning journal entry from yesterday in a flat voice: *I am calm. I am abundant. I am loved.* She reads it expressionlessly. Time-lapse to evening, same kitchen, journal again: *I screamed at my kid. I haven't slept. I feel like I am failing.* Time-lapse to the next morning: *I am calm. I am abundant. I am loved.* The cycle repeats four times. End card: "Coaching is what you do BETWEEN the affirmations." App name underneath, small.

  No product shown. The performance of the affirmation IS the manufactured surface. The truthful middle is what the app holds.

  **B. Same category, real brand: Nature's Shimmer Watercolor Workshop, $47.**

  The category-default for art courses is to show finished masterpieces. The Dove move is to show how those finished pieces are manufactured.

  Ninety seconds. A still camera in a kitchen. A woman sits with a blank page. Time-lapse: she paints. The first wash is muddy. She walks away. She comes back. She paints again over the dry first layer. Better but still off. She walks away. The time-lapse keeps going across what is clearly days. The same kitchen, light moving. Pages accumulating. By the end of the film the page on the table is finished and luminous. End card: "Most watercolors that look effortless took six visits to the table. We teach the visits, not the finish."

  Workshop name in italic serif. Tiny. No price. No "you will learn." The reveal is the entire pitch.

  The mechanism is identical. The audience has been measuring themselves against finished art they did not know was made over days. Showing them the actual manufacturing process collapses the impossible standard. The brand becomes the one who told them the truth about how art is actually made.

A note on why Evolution still works almost twenty years later: the manufacturing has only gotten more elaborate. In 2006 it was hair and Photoshop. In 2026 it is hair, Photoshop, AI rendering, algorithmic filtering, generative replacement of entire faces, and synthetic reels. The standard being measured against is more manufactured than ever, which means the format that reveals the manufacturing has more material to work with than ever.

The discipline is showing the manufacturing without becoming preachy about it. Evolution does not lecture. The camera simply runs. The reveal happens in the viewer's own mind. That is what makes it stick.

What to steal: pick the one standard your audience is silently exhausted by. Document, in real time, the actual process that produces that standard. Withhold your product entirely. Let the audience walk away knowing how the sausage is made. Your brand becomes the one who told them.

What to leave behind: the Photoshop screen recording trick. The reveal in 2026 has to use a 2026 vocabulary. AI generators, filter overlays, time-lapse against generated content. The format has updated. The mechanism is the same.
